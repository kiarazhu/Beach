Safety Signs and Flags

{{text[0]}} To swim safely, swim between the flags, with a friend. Don’t swim in water that is deeper or rougher than you are comfortable. You can ask the lifeguards for information about the surf conditions if you are unsure!

{{text[1]}} Beaches in Australia have signs to help you understand whether it is safe to swim or not:

{{flags["RedYellow"]}} Red & Yellow Flags: Swim between the flags. This area will be safe to swim in. {{flags["Red"]}} Red Flag: No Swimming.
{{flags["Yellow"]}} Yellow Flag: Caution required. Potential hazards. {{flags["RedWhite"]}} Red & White Flag: Evacuate the water.
{{flags["BlackWhite"]}} Black & White Flag: Surfcraft riding area boundary.
{{warnings["Warning"]}} Warning {{warnings["NoSwim"]}} Swimming not advised {{warnings["Waves"]}} Large waves {{warnings["Stingers"]}} Marine Stingers

{{text[2]}} If you are in trouble in the water then you can signal for help from a lifeguard by raising your arm and waving it from side to side.

{{text[3]}} Australian beaches can contain different wildlife species, some of which can be dangerous. If it is alive, give it space.
